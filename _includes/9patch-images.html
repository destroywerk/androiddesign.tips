9-patch images

Fill area Stretchable area

9-patch images can seem like a complicated beast, but once you’ve grasped it they’re incredibly useful. By saving out a png as [name].9.png Android recognises it as a 9-patch file and will use the 1-pixel #000 guides to scale the image and define the content area.

The top and left are the stretchable areas and the bottom and right are how to define where the content will fill. You need to bear in mind that the 1-pixel guide lines MUST be #000000 and at the edges of your image boundary and the rest of that 1px edge must be COMPLETELY transparent. Even 1 anti-aliased half pixel will cause the .9.png to fail.
